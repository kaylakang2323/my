Hierarchy is often designed to enable coordination at scale.

However, it also introduces predictable distortion into organizational signals.

As hierarchy increases, feedback quality becomes increasingly asymmetric. Information flows upward with filtration, while decisions flow downward with compression.

This creates three structural distortions:

Critical signals from lower levels are softened or lost.

Decision rationale becomes detached from execution reality.

Feedback loops become slower and less accurate.

The result is not simply slower communication, but reduced signal fidelity.

Organizations that ignore this effect interpret hierarchy as neutral structure.

In reality, hierarchy is an information transformation system that alters the quality of what the organization perceives.
