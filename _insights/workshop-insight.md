Workshops are commonly framed as collaborative spaces where alignment emerges through discussion.

This framing is incomplete.

A well-designed workshop is not a conversation. It is a structured extraction system for organizational intent.

The purpose is not participation, but transformation of distributed knowledge into structured decisions.

Without structure, workshops amplify noise:
dominant voices shape outcomes, ambiguity persists, and outputs remain informal.

With structure, workshops become mechanisms that:
- constrain input types
- sequence cognitive divergence and convergence
- convert discussion into decision artifacts

The effectiveness of a workshop is not measured by engagement, but by the quality of extracted structure.

Collaboration is a byproduct. Extraction is the function.
