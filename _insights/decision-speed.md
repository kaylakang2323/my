Speed is often treated as a universal indicator of organizational health.

However, mature organizations recognize that not all decisions should be optimized for speed.

Premature decisions create downstream costs that exceed the benefit of rapid execution.

Effective organizations introduce deliberate friction into decision pathways.

This friction serves three functions:

It forces clarification of assumptions before action.

It prevents irreversible commitments based on incomplete information.

It improves the quality of alignment across stakeholders.

Slowness, in this context, is not inefficiency. It is a control mechanism.

The goal is not to slow everything down, but to slow the right decisions at the right time.
