## Overview

As our organization continued to expand its product portfolio, different business units often interpreted customer value differently when proposing new solutions.

Although the concept of a Customer Value Proposition (CVP) already existed, there was no consistent process to validate whether proposed products genuinely addressed customer needs before entering development.

I led the effort to transform this concept into an operational governance process that became part of the organization's product development lifecycle.

---

## Business Context

Product planning was traditionally driven by business ideas and functional requirements.

Without a structured validation process, customer value was often interpreted differently across teams, creating inconsistencies in product direction and increasing the risk of building solutions that lacked clear customer value.

The opportunity was to introduce a governance model that aligned business, customer experience, product management, and engineering around a shared understanding of customer value before development began.

---

## Organizational Challenge

The challenge was not creating another review meeting.

The organization needed a repeatable decision-making process that could:

- Define Customer Value Propositions (CVPs) consistently
- Validate customer value before development investment
- Encourage cross-functional discussion early in the product lifecycle
- Improve decision quality through structured review
- Create organizational alignment around customer-centered thinking

The goal was to make customer value validation an integral part of how products were planned—not an optional design activity.

---

## My Role

I led the initiative from a governance and facilitation perspective by:

- Designing the operational workflow for Customer Value Proposition (CVP) validation
- Embedding the governance process into the product development lifecycle
- Establishing a cross-functional CVP review board
- Facilitating workshops and review sessions across business units
- Coordinating collaboration between Business, CX, Product Management, Engineering, and leadership
- Defining review criteria and governance procedures
- Supporting business teams through coaching when proposals required refinement

---

## Approach

Rather than positioning Customer Value Proposition (CVP) as a design deliverable, we integrated it into the organization's decision-making process.

Every new product initiative was required to complete Customer Value Proposition validation before moving forward.

The governance process brought together representatives from:

- Business
- Customer Experience Designer(CX)
- Product Management
- Engineering
- Business Leadership

Each proposal was reviewed through a structured evaluation process.

Outcomes included:

- Approval to proceed
- Request for revision
- Coaching and refinement before resubmission

By combining structured governance with collaborative facilitation, customer value became a shared organizational responsibility rather than the responsibility of a single team.

---

## Organizational Impact

The initiative fundamentally changed how new products were planned across the organization.

Key outcomes included:

- Customer Value Proposition validation became a mandatory step in product planning
- A cross-functional governance board was established and continues to operate
- Product teams adopted a shared framework for defining customer value
- Customer-centered thinking became embedded within business planning
- Cross-functional collaboration improved before development began
- The governance model has remained in operation for multiple years

---

## What Changed?

### Before

- Business units defined customer value independently.
- Product ideas entered development without consistent validation.
- Cross-functional discussion often occurred after key decisions had already been made.

### After

- Customer Value Proposition validation became a required governance process.
- Cross-functional stakeholders evaluated product proposals together.
- Business teams received structured feedback and coaching before development.
- Customer value became a shared decision-making criterion across the organization.

---

## Key Learnings

Creating a customer-centered organization requires more than encouraging customer-centric thinking.

Sustainable transformation happens when customer value becomes part of the organization's operating model.

By embedding Customer Value Proposition validation into governance rather than treating it as a design exercise, the organization established a repeatable process that continues to shape product decisions years after its introduction.
