## Overview

As the organization expanded globally, there was a growing need to establish and scale design capabilities across multiple regions in Asia, including India, Vietnam, and China.

Rather than operating as a centralized design team, the organization required distributed teams that could function independently while maintaining consistent standards, governance, and collaboration models.

I led the setup and scaling of global design teams across these regions.

---

## Business Context

Global expansion introduced several structural challenges:

- Lack of local design teams in key regions
- Dependence on centralized decision-making
- Limited alignment between headquarters and local execution
- Inconsistent hiring and capability standards across regions

To support business growth, localized design capabilities needed to be established and operationalized.

---

## Organizational Challenge

The core challenge was building scalable global design capability, not just hiring individuals.

This required:

- Establishing design teams in new regions
- Defining hiring and competency standards
- Recruiting design managers and individual contributors
- Building governance structures for distributed teams
- Ensuring alignment between HQ and regional teams
- Enabling consistent decision-making across geographies

---

## My Role

I led the end-to-end global scaling initiative by:

- Defining the structure of regional design organizations
- Establishing design teams in India, Vietnam, and China
- Leading recruitment of design managers and team members
- Designing onboarding and operational processes for each region
- Setting up weekly governance and reporting structures
- Supporting local decision-making while maintaining alignment with HQ strategy
- Acting as a bridge between headquarters and regional teams

---

## Approach

The approach focused on balancing autonomy and alignment across distributed teams.

Key elements included:

- Creating regional team structures tailored to local contexts
- Recruiting and onboarding design leaders in each region
- Establishing weekly governance rhythms for coordination
- Providing decision-making support to regional leads
- Standardizing core design principles while allowing local execution flexibility
- Ensuring continuous communication between HQ and regional teams

The goal was not central control, but scalable coordination.

---

## Organizational Impact

The initiative resulted in:

- Establishment of design organizations in India, Vietnam, and China
- Operational global design network across Asia
- Improved hiring speed and regional capability building
- Structured governance model for distributed teams
- Stronger alignment between HQ and regional execution
- Sustainable scaling model for future expansion

---

## What Changed?

### Before

- Design work was centralized at headquarters
- Regional execution depended heavily on HQ decisions
- Hiring and capability building were inconsistent across regions

### After

- Distributed design organizations were established across Asia
- Regional teams operated with defined autonomy
- Governance structures ensured alignment across regions
- Hiring and team building became localized and scalable

---

## Key Learnings

Scaling global organizations requires more than replication of central teams.

Sustainable growth comes from designing structures that balance autonomy, alignment, and governance across regions while enabling local execution capabilities.
