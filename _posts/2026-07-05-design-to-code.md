## Overview

As our organization matured, design-to-development handoff became a growing operational challenge. Designers produced high-fidelity Figma files, but engineers still recreated interfaces manually, making delivery slower and less consistent.

Commercial Design-to-Code solutions were available, but adopting the required enterprise Figma capabilities would have resulted in approximately KRW 1.7 billion in additional licensing costs.

Rather than purchasing an expensive commercial solution, I proposed building an internal Design-to-Code platform designed specifically for our organization's workflow and constraints.

---

## Business Context

The organization was pursuing greater development efficiency while preparing for more automated product delivery.

Although commercial Design-to-Code products existed, two major constraints limited adoption:

- Enterprise licensing costs were prohibitively expensive.
- Headquarters engineering resources were difficult to secure due to competing priorities.

The challenge became finding a practical alternative that balanced cost, scalability, and engineering efficiency.

---

## Organizational Challenge

The objective was not simply to generate code from design files.

The organization needed an operating model that could:

- Reduce repetitive handoff between design and engineering
- Improve implementation consistency
- Minimize additional software licensing costs
- Work within existing organizational constraints
- Create a scalable foundation for future automation

Instead of adapting our process to fit an external product, we chose to design a solution around our own delivery workflow.

---

## My Role

I led the initiative from a strategy and workflow perspective by:

- Identifying Design-to-Code as a strategic transformation opportunity
- Evaluating commercial solutions and their cost implications
- Defining the concept and operating model for an internal Design-to-Code platform
- Designing the end-to-end workflow connecting Figma, engineering, and GitHub
- Leveraging the newly established Vietnam organization to secure development capacity
- Coordinating implementation across distributed teams
- Aligning stakeholders across design and engineering throughout the project

---

## Approach

Rather than requesting additional headquarters engineering resources, I utilized engineering capacity within the Vietnam organization that I had helped establish.

Working with the distributed team, we developed an internal Figma plugin that could:

- Convert Figma design assets into production-ready code
- Automatically deliver generated code into GitHub repositories
- Reduce repetitive engineering work
- Standardize the design-to-development handoff process

Instead of replicating every capability offered by commercial platforms, we focused on solving the organization's highest-impact workflow challenges.

The result was a lightweight internal platform that aligned with our operating model while avoiding significant licensing costs.

---

## Organizational Impact

The initiative demonstrated how workflow redesign and organizational collaboration could produce both operational and financial value.

Key outcomes included:

- Avoided approximately KRW 1.7 billion in additional software licensing costs
- Established an internal Design-to-Code platform
- Automated design-to-code conversion through a custom Figma plugin
- Integrated generated code directly into GitHub-based development workflows
- Reduced repetitive engineering effort during UI implementation
- Strengthened collaboration between design and engineering teams
- Created a scalable foundation for future automation initiatives

---

## What Changed?

### Before

- Designers handed off Figma files manually.
- Engineers recreated UI components by hand.
- Commercial automation required substantial licensing investment.
- Headquarters engineering resources were difficult to secure.

### After

- Design assets were automatically transformed into production-ready code.
- Generated code flowed directly into GitHub repositories.
- Manual implementation effort was significantly reduced.
- Existing global resources were leveraged to build an internal platform.
- Automation became achievable without major additional software investment.

---

## Key Learnings

Successful technology transformation is driven as much by organizational design as by technical implementation.

Rather than relying on larger budgets or additional headcount, this initiative demonstrated how workflow redesign, strategic resource allocation, and cross-functional collaboration can create scalable internal capabilities.

The most effective solutions are often those designed around how an organization actually operates—not around the limitations or pricing models of commercial tools.
