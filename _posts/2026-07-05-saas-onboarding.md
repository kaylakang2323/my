## Overview

As the organization transitioned from on-premise solutions to SaaS-based offerings, the onboarding experience became a critical factor in user adoption and product success.

Existing onboarding flows were fragmented across business units and lacked a unified structure, making it difficult for users to understand and activate the product effectively.

I led the design and implementation of a unified SaaS onboarding experience, working across business, CX, product, and engineering teams.

---

## Business Context

The shift to SaaS required a fundamental change in how users were introduced to and activated within the product ecosystem.

However, the existing onboarding experience:

- Was inconsistent across business units
- Lacked clear user guidance during initial setup
- Required users to navigate multiple disconnected workflows
- Did not reflect a unified product narrative

A more structured and scalable onboarding experience was required to support SaaS adoption.

---

## Organizational Challenge

The challenge was not only UX design, but alignment across multiple stakeholders.

The onboarding experience needed to:

- Guide users from initial access to successful activation
- Align multiple business units into a single user journey
- Translate complex enterprise workflows into intuitive UX
- Support scalable SaaS adoption across regions and teams
- Be implemented within existing system constraints

---

## My Role

I owned the end-to-end onboarding experience design and implementation by:

- Designing the SaaS onboarding user journey
- Conducting cross-functional design thinking workshops to surface requirements
- Aligning business units, CX teams, PMs, and engineering stakeholders
- Translating business requirements into UX flows and interface design
- Leading onboarding screen design and interaction structure
- Supporting implementation through iterative feedback cycles

---

## Approach

I applied a structured design thinking approach to align fragmented business requirements into a unified onboarding experience.

Key steps included:

- Running cross-functional workshops to extract user and business needs
- Mapping onboarding journeys across different business units
- Defining a unified onboarding flow for SaaS users
- Designing onboarding screens and interaction logic
- Iterating with CX, PM, and engineering teams during implementation

The focus was on creating a coherent user journey rather than isolated feature screens.

---

## Organizational Impact

The initiative resulted in a unified onboarding experience that:

- Improved clarity of SaaS product activation flow
- Reduced fragmentation across business units
- Established a standardized onboarding structure
- Improved collaboration between CX, PM, and engineering teams
- Served as a foundational UX model for subsequent SaaS products

---

## What Changed?

### Before

- Onboarding experiences varied by business unit
- Users faced fragmented activation flows
- Product understanding was inconsistent across regions

### After

- A unified SaaS onboarding journey was established
- Users followed a structured activation flow
- Cross-functional alignment improved around onboarding UX
- Onboarding became a standardized product experience layer

---

## Key Learnings

Enterprise SaaS transformation requires more than feature redesign.

Successful onboarding experiences depend on aligning organizational stakeholders around a unified user journey and translating complex system structures into intuitive user flows.
