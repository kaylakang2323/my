## Overview

As our design organization expanded, designers primarily focused on execution after requirements had already been defined.

While product quality continued to improve, opportunities to influence business strategy and customer value were often missed.

Rather than simply improving existing design processes, I proposed creating a new organizational role that could bridge business, customers, and design teams.

This initiative evolved into the **Design Consulting** role.

---

## Business Context

The organization needed stronger collaboration between business units and the design team.

Customer needs were often interpreted differently across departments, resulting in late design involvement and limited opportunities for strategic influence.

The challenge was not about hiring more designers—it was about redefining how design contributed to business decisions.

---

## Organizational Challenge

Creating a new role required more than assigning a different job title.

The organization needed a clear operating model that defined:

- Why the role existed
- Responsibilities and ownership
- Required competencies
- Career progression
- Performance evaluation
- Collaboration with existing teams

Without these foundations, the role would remain ambiguous and difficult to scale.

---

## My Role

I led the organizational design of the new role by:

- Defining the Design Consulting operating model
- Designing role descriptions and responsibilities
- Building competency frameworks
- Designing career progression criteria
- Establishing performance and peer review systems
- Facilitating alignment across stakeholders

---

## Approach

The role was designed to engage earlier in the product lifecycle.

Instead of receiving completed requirements, Design Consultants worked directly with business stakeholders to:

- Discover customer needs
- Facilitate Design Thinking workshops
- Identify business opportunities
- Translate customer insights into product direction
- Align stakeholders before implementation

This shifted design from a delivery function to a strategic partner.

---

## Organizational Impact

The initiative established a repeatable organizational model for customer-centered collaboration.

Key outcomes included:

- Creation of a new Design Consulting role
- Competency framework and career ladder
- Performance and peer evaluation system
- Earlier design involvement in business discussions
- Stronger collaboration between business and design teams

---

## What Changed?

### Before

- Designers participated after requirements were defined.
- Customer understanding depended on business teams.
- Design focused primarily on execution.

### After

- Design Consultants participated during problem discovery.
- Customer insights became a shared responsibility.
- Design contributed to business strategy and product direction.

---

## Key Learnings

Organizational transformation rarely begins with new technology.

Sometimes the most effective way to improve customer outcomes is to redesign the roles, responsibilities, and collaboration models that connect people across the organization.

Creating a new role was ultimately about creating a new way of working.
