## Overview

Traditional user research is constrained by recruitment effort, scheduling limitations, incentive management, and the availability of participants.

As a result, teams often face trade-offs between research depth, speed, and scale, especially during early product exploration and iterative testing phases.

I proposed an AI agent-based user research model designed to extend research capacity beyond these practical constraints by simulating structured personas and enabling scalable qualitative exploration.

---

## Business Context

User research in product development typically requires recruiting real participants that match predefined personas.

This process is often:

- Time-consuming due to recruitment cycles
- Constrained by budget and incentive policies
- Limited by geographical and scheduling constraints
- Difficult to scale across multiple personas simultaneously

These constraints reduce the speed at which teams can validate ideas and explore alternative product directions.

---

## Organizational Challenge

The core challenge was not the absence of research practices, but the inability to scale them effectively.

The organization needed a way to:

- Rapidly explore multiple personas in parallel
- Conduct early-stage interviews without logistical overhead
- Identify pain points before prototype development
- Validate usability hypotheses faster
- Reduce dependency on physical participant recruitment for early discovery phases

---

## My Role

I initiated and proposed an AI-based research augmentation model by:

- Defining the concept of persona-driven AI research agents
- Mapping the end-to-end user research workflow for automation opportunities
- Designing a model for simulating structured user personas at scale
- Proposing the integration of AI agents into early-stage product discovery
- Aligning the concept with product, CX, and research stakeholders
- Contributing to the conceptual foundation that later informed a patent application by the team

---

## Approach

The proposed model replaces early-stage manual research bottlenecks with AI-generated research agents based on defined personas.

The workflow includes:

- Defining structured personas based on target user segments
- Generating AI agents that simulate these personas
- Conducting simulated interviews to surface pain points
- Exploring behavioral patterns and decision-making logic
- Testing early product concepts and prototypes
- Aggregating insights for refinement before real-user validation

Rather than replacing human users, the system is designed to extend research capacity during early discovery phases, where speed and iteration are critical.

---

## Organizational Impact

While still in a conceptual and early validation stage, the proposal led to:

- Internal adoption of AI-assisted user research experimentation
- Development of prototype AI agent-based research workflows
- Pilot testing within selected product teams
- Contribution to a team-level patent application related to AI-assisted research methods
- Increased organizational awareness of scalable research methodologies

---

## What Changed?

### Before

- User research depended entirely on recruited participants.
- Early-stage validation was limited by time and logistics.
- Persona exploration was constrained by available sample size.

### After

- AI agents enabled scalable simulation of user personas.
- Early-stage insights could be generated before recruiting real users.
- Research exploration expanded across multiple personas in parallel.
- Teams could iterate faster during discovery and concept validation phases.

---

## Key Learnings

AI does not replace user research—it expands its boundary conditions.

By introducing AI agent-based simulation into the research workflow, it becomes possible to explore hypotheses earlier, reduce dependency on logistical constraints, and improve the efficiency of downstream validation with real users.

This approach represents a shift from traditional linear user research to a hybrid model combining AI simulation and human validation.
